# Learning Loop

This lab uses a weekly operating cadence to keep exploration structured without turning it into a fixed syllabus.

## Weekly Cadence

1. Select one or two questions from the current tracks or backlog.
2. Run investigations by reading, tracing, comparing, or reviewing systems.
3. Capture findings in track notes, system notes, diagrams, or journal entries.
4. Turn promising findings into small experiments or architecture reviews.
5. End the week by updating open questions, follow-ups, and backlog items.

Tracks can change when investigations produce better questions.

## Outputs

- Investigation notes
- Runtime or system traces
- Architecture diagrams
- Experiment writeups
- Project decisions
- Backlog updates

## Principles

- Follow evidence from real systems and traces.
- Prefer small, inspectable experiments over broad plans.
- Keep tracks provisional and revise them when better questions emerge.
- Preserve uncertainty rather than forcing premature conclusions.
